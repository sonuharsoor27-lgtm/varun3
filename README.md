Key Aspects of Medical Stores:
•	Types: Standalone shops (residential areas), hospital pharmacies, chain pharmacies (e.g., Apollo Pharmacy, Medplus), and township stores.
•	Requirements: Minimum area of 10 sq. meters for retail, 15 sq. meters for wholesale/retail combo, and a registered pharmacist for dispensing.
•	Inventory: They stock "ethical" (branded) and generic medicines.
•	Consumer Rights: Customers should receive an itemized bill (listing patient name, doctor, batch number, expiry date, and GST) and ensure medicine validity.
•	Profit Margins: Generic medicines can offer 40% to 50% margins, while branded medicine profits vary by company.

Important Considerations:
•	Location: High-visibility areas with few competitors are ideal.
•	Licenses: Required, including Drug License and Goods & Services Tax (GST) registration.
•	Cold Chain: Proper storage for vaccines and insulin is mandatory.
